We've audited hundreds of small business websites. The same five mistakes keep appearing. Here they are — with the exact fixes.

## 1. Missing Title Tags

Every page needs a unique, descriptive `<title>` tag. Yet 30% of sites we audit have duplicate or missing titles.

**The fix:** Write a unique title for each page. Format: "Page Topic | Business Name." Keep it under 60 characters.

## 2. No Structured Data

JSON-LD structured data is the highest-impact SEO fix for most small businesses. It tells Google your business name, address, services, hours, and reviews — in a format Google understands perfectly.

**The fix:** Add Organization or LocalBusiness schema to your homepage. Add FAQ schema to your FAQ page. Add Article schema to blog posts.

## 3. Broken Internal Links

Pages that aren't linked from anywhere on your site are orphan pages. Google can't find them. Users can't find them either.

**The fix:** Audit your internal links. Make sure every page is reachable from at least one other page. Use descriptive anchor text.

## 4. Missing Alt Text on Images

Alt text serves two purposes: accessibility and SEO. Google uses it to understand what images show.

**The fix:** Add descriptive alt text to every image. Don't keyword-stuff — describe what's actually in the image.

## 5. No XML Sitemap

A sitemap helps Google discover all your pages. Without it, Google relies entirely on following links — which means orphan pages stay invisible.

**The fix:** Generate a sitemap.xml. Submit it to Google Search Console. Update it when you add new pages.
