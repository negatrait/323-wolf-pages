SEO gets all the attention. But there's a second layer of search visibility that most small businesses completely ignore: **Geographic Optimization (GEO).**

## What Is GEO?

GEO is the practice of optimizing your online presence so your business appears in location-based searches: "bakery near me," "plumber in Helsinki," "restaurant open now."

These searches trigger Google's local pack — the map with three businesses at the top of results. If you're not in the local pack, you're invisible to local customers.

## Three Pillars of GEO

### 1. Google Business Profile

Your GBP is the single most important local ranking factor. If you don't have one, or if it's incomplete, you won't show up in Maps or the local pack.

**Check:** Is your profile verified? Are all fields filled in? Do you have photos, categories, and business hours?

### 2. NAP Consistency

Your Name, Address, and Phone number must be identical everywhere: your website, Google, social media, directories. Even small differences (St. vs Street) hurt your rankings.

**Check:** Search for your business name + city. Are all listings consistent?

### 3. Local Content

Pages targeting your service areas, local landing pages, and FAQ content with local keywords signal to Google that you serve a specific geography.

**Check:** Do you have pages for each area you serve?

## Quick GEO Checklist

- Google Business Profile verified and complete
- NAP consistent across all platforms
- Local landing pages for service areas
- Reviews on Google (ask customers)
- Schema markup with business address
